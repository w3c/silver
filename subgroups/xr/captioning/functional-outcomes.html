Our first draft of Functional Outcomes are below. The wording of these still needs to be altered but the overall concepts are correct.

Potential Tag ideas are taken from the Silver Ideas for Tagging Document.

Outcome 1: We Need Captions

Auditory information, including speech and key sound effects, are translated into alternative formats (e.g. captions) so media can be consumed when sound is unavailable or limited

Existing Success Criteria (Percievable)

  1. Success Criteria 1.2.2 - Captions (Prerecorded)
  2. Success Criterion 1.2.3 Audio Description or Media Alternative (Prerecorded)
  3. Success Criterion 1.2.4 Captions (Live)
  4. Success Criterion 1.2.6 Sign Language (Prerecorded)
  5. Success Criterion 1.2.7 Extended Audio Description (Prerecorded)

    6. Potential Tags

    • Principles - Perceivable
    • Technology - Web, VR, AR, XR.
    • Semantic Meaning and Context - Unsure
    • Activity - Copy and Content Creation, Development?
    • Functional Need -
      • Usage without hearing
      • Usage with limited hearing
      • Usage without vision
      • Usage with limited vision

Outcome 2: We need meta data of sound effects

Auditory meta-information, including sound directionality, is conveyed to the viewer to that contextual information is available when sound is unavailable or limited

Existing Success Criteria (Percievable)

  1. Success Criterion 1.3.1 Info and Relationships
  2. Success Criterion 1.3.3 Sensory Characteristics

    • Usage with Limited Reach

Outcome 3: Second screen adaptions should be possible

Captions and caption meta-data are capable of being presented in alternative methods (e.g. second screen) to make the information more accessible when visual access is unavailable

Outcome 4: Customization of captions

Customisation of caption style and position is available to support users that would benefit from tailored presentation options

Outcome 5: Time alterations for caption viewing

The amount of time that a given caption (and associated meta-data) spends on screen can be personalised in order to give additional time to locate the sound that is being presented
