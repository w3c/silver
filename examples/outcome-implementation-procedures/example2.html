Example outcome evaluation procedure: Provide help and support (large orgs)

Note: This example shows an outcome implementation procedure that describes how a large organization with significant web resources and more complicated web development processes could meaningfully implement the COGA Usable guidance. Time investment, define roles, and evaluation requirements are higher in this example, but are meant to be achievable for organizations of the target size.

Note: All content here is provide for example purposes only. Only the overall structure is meant for review.

2. Accessibility guidance

Developers in all roles use cognitive accessibility guidance to Provide Help and Support published as a supplement to WCAG 2.

Note: The definition of "use" could change based on the conformance level. For lower levels of conformance, we're primary looking at the proedures an organization has put in place to measure a protocol. These controls help assure users that guidance is being followed, though outputs are not normally measured at this level. At higher conformance levels the output of the procedure implementation will be measured with varying degrees of rigour depending on the level of conformance claimed. All protocols will have evaluation measures. All results of evaluations must be publicy documented at higher conformance levels. At lower conformance levels, documentation of procedures is sufficent.

3. Implementation procedures

Note: For a large organization, the procedures would be more specific and comprehensive about steps needed. This is just a starter example to differentiate from the small org example.

All roles read the accessibility guidance.

Management

Management oversees organizational commitment to implementing this outcome implementation procedure. Initially this is expected to take .4 FTE until all requirements for initial implementation have been met. .025 FTE is expected for ongoing supervision and support.

Web department managers

Web department managers develop requirements and strategies for implementation, determining what additions and changes are needed on the site, and setting requirements for design and placement of solutions. Initially this is expected to take .4 FTE for each manager until all requirements for initial implementation have been met. .1 FTE is expected for ongoing supervision and support.

Designers

@@ Initially this is expected to take .8 FTE for each designer until all requirements for initial implementation have been met. .05 FTE is expected for ongoing support.

Coders

@@ .05 FTE for each coder

Accessibility department

The accessibility department coordinates with management in the strategy for following these procedures, evaluates progress and barriers, and provides relevant information on the company’s accessibility statement. .1 FTE during coordination points.

Risk management department

The risk management department monitors overall progress and effectiveness reports in order to be able to respond to complaints quickly and effectively. .05 FTE during coordination points.

4. Evaluation procedures

The report describing attention points serves as a milestone that the procedure has been implemented.

Frequency and duration of meetings related to the task are tracked for a given annual period. Frequency should be 12 / year, duration should be 1 hour / meeting. The score is the actual vs benchmark ratio.

We use the following evaluation procedures in the newly-released Help and Support Evaluation Procedure published as a companion to the Making Contnet Usable Protocol:

  • We have evaluated all content and ensured compliance with all relavant WCAG 3.0 methods and maintain a X conformance level for all web support offerings. See the results from our latest audit.
  • We offer live telephone support. To ensure all staff are properly trained in accessible communication methods, we require staff to take X curriculum and test all staff twice a year. Staff are required to acheive a score of at least X to serve customers.
  • We hire people with disabilties sourced by X company to test our support processes annually. X company provides an indepdentant report and we remediate any findings from that testing within 90 days.
  • To ensure even greater accessibility, Company X has developed Open A11y 100 software. This software lints code to help software developers implement new W3C standards, including ones related to personalization and content pronounciation.
  • To ensure we meet the needs of everyone, we annually have X Company, who specializes in conformance beyond WCAG 3.0 methods to audit our web help options. See the results from our latest audit. All findings are remediated within 180 days.
  • For any customer who is unable to obtain support through our Help line or Portal, our company provides trained Customer Success Agents who work with the customer directly in the format they prefer. Our company records logs from those support cases and uses them to inform the design of new Help Portal features and in staff training/assessments.

5. Scoring information

When implementation is demonstrated with the initial report, the relevant outcomes score at the lowest level of conformance.

Note: higher conformance levels would require additional evaluation of the implementation procedures, beyond the baseline.
